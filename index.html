Common Mental Health Issues

Learn about the most common mental health challenges, their signs, and gentle ways to cope.

Anxiety

Persistent feelings of nervousness, worry, or fear that can affect daily life. Anxiety can manifest physically and emotionally, and may include panic attacks or social anxiety.

  • Symptoms: Excessive worry, restlessness, rapid heartbeat.
  • Tips: Practice deep breathing, talk to someone you trust.

When to seek help: If anxiety disrupts your daily life.

Depression

A prolonged state of sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness. Depression can cause changes in sleep, appetite, motivation, and self-esteem, and may make daily tasks feel overwhelming.

  • Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, fatigue.
  • Tips: Stay connected, set small goals, seek sunlight.

When to seek help: If symptoms last more than two weeks or are severe.

Stress

Emotional and physical strain caused by challenging or demanding situations. Stress can be acute or chronic, and may impact your mood, body, and ability to cope with daily life.

  • Symptoms: Feeling overwhelmed, headaches, trouble sleeping.
  • Tips: Take breaks, move your body, talk it out.

When to seek help: If stress feels unmanageable or constant.

PTSD

A mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing trauma. PTSD can cause flashbacks, nightmares, and intense emotional distress, often long after the traumatic event.

  • Symptoms: Flashbacks, avoidance, feeling on edge.
  • Tips: Practice grounding, seek safe spaces, talk to a professional.

When to seek help: If symptoms persist or disrupt daily life.

OCD

Characterized by unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) performed to reduce anxiety. OCD can be time-consuming and distressing.

  • Symptoms: Obsessions, compulsions, distress when unable to perform rituals.
  • Tips: Practice self-compassion, try gentle exposure, seek support.

When to seek help: If OCD interferes with daily life or causes distress.

Bipolar Disorder

A condition marked by extreme mood swings, including emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). Bipolar disorder can affect energy, judgment, and relationships.

  • Symptoms: Mood swings, high energy, sadness, impulsivity.
  • Tips: Keep a routine, track moods, reach out for help.

When to seek help: If mood changes are severe or impact daily life.
